## Key Highlights

- India imports roughly 85% of its crude oil requirement; international disruptions have a direct domestic effect.
- The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint; any disruption pushes crude prices higher.
- Analysts indicate crude oil prices above USD 100–120/barrel typically translate to higher retail petrol prices in India.
- Estimated petrol price increase range (based on current trends): INR 5–15 per litre; actual outcome depends on taxes, subsidies, and exchange rate movements.
- Rising fuel costs propagate through transport and logistics, contributing to broader inflation.
